Massif du Siroua Trek

4 Days Moderate Siroua Mountains

Overview

Step off the beaten path and into the otherworldly landscapes of the Massif du Siroua. Bridging the gap between the towering High Atlas and the arid Anti-Atlas, this ancient volcanic range offers a hiking experience unlike any other in Morocco.

Your journey will take you through dramatic gorges, eroded rock formations, and terraced fields where the precious saffron crocus blooms. You’ll walk through remote Berber villages where time seems to stand still, meeting locals whose warm hospitality is as legendary as the landscape itself. This is more than a trek; it's a deep dive into the heart of rural Morocco. You can read more about the unique Berber villages in our guide.

Highlights

  • Summit Mount Siroua (3304m) for panoramic views.
  • Discover the "Red Gold" in the saffron capital of Taliouine.
  • Explore unique volcanic pillars and rock towers.
  • Tea ceremonies in authentic Berber homes.

Itinerary

Day 1 Marrakech to Taliouine & Tamlakout

We leave Marrakech early, crossing the Tizi n'Tichka pass. We stop in Taliouine, the heart of saffron country, to visit a cooperative before heading to the village of Tamlakout (1650m) to meet our muleteer team.

2 hrs walk • 5 hrs drive
Day 2 Tamlakout to Ait Tigga

Our trek begins in earnest. We hike through arid plateaus and seasonal riverbeds, passing ancient granaries (igoudar) perched on cliffs. We camp near the village of Ait Tigga.

5-6 hrs walking
Day 3 Ait Tigga to Tajgalte

We ascend through terraced fields and almond groves. The landscape changes as we approach the foot of the Siroua peak, setting up camp at the green pastures of Tajgalte.

6 hrs walking
Day 4 Summit of Mount Siroua (3304m)

The big day. We climb steadily to the twin peaks of Siroua. The final scramble is optional but rewarding, offering views from the High Atlas to the Sahara. We descend to camp at Iriri.

7 hrs walking • Ascent: 800m
Day 5 Iriri to Azib n'Ouagane

A relaxing walk through the Amassine valley, known for its dramatic rock formations that look like a giant's playground. We camp near the summer pastures of Azib n'Ouagane.

4-5 hrs walking
Day 6 Return to Marrakech

A short morning walk back to the road where our transport awaits. We drive back to Marrakech, arriving in the late afternoon.

2 hrs walk • 5 hrs drive

Know Before You Go

  • Fitness: Good stamina required. Long walking days.
  • Weather: Can be cold at night. Bring layers.
  • Gear: Sturdy hiking boots are essential.
