The Happy Valley

A\u00eft Bougmez Cultural & Nature Trek

What You're Going To Do

Cultural and nature-focused trek in the A\u00eft Bougmez valley, featuring villages, agriculture, Berber life, and easy scenic hikes. Known as the "Happy Valley" for its welcoming people and lush landscapes, this trek offers an authentic immersion into traditional Moroccan mountain culture.

Walk through apple orchards and terraced fields, visit ancient granaries with 360° views, discover dinosaur tracks dating back millions of years, and spend nights in traditional Berber gîtes where hospitality is a way of life.

Highlights

  • Apple orchards and terraced agricultural fields
  • Sidi Moussa granary with 360° panoramic views
  • Dinosaur tracks at Ibakliwin (Jurassic period)
  • Nights in traditional Berber gîtes
  • Meet local artisans and farmers
  • Share mint tea and traditional meals with families

Day-by-Day Itinerary

Day 1: Marrakech → Agouti → Tabant

Depart Marrakech and drive through the scenic Ouzoud region. Arrive in Agouti village and begin a gentle trek through walnut groves and terraced fields to Tabant, the valley's main village. Settle into a traditional gîte and enjoy a home-cooked Berber dinner.

Trekking: 2-3 hours | Elevation: Minimal

Day 2: Tabant → Ibakliwin → Dinosaur Tracks

Trek to the village of Ibakliwin, famous for its well-preserved dinosaur footprints embedded in rock formations. Your local guide will explain the geological history. Continue through scenic valleys with views of traditional kasbahs. Overnight in a family-run gîte.

Trekking: 4-5 hours | Highlights: Dinosaur tracks, kasbahs

Day 3: Arouss Village → Sidi Moussa Granary

Hike to Arouss village and climb to the ancient Sidi Moussa granary (agadir), perched on a hilltop with breathtaking 360° views of the entire valley and surrounding peaks. Learn about traditional grain storage methods. Descend and spend the evening with a local family, experiencing authentic Berber hospitality.

Trekking: 3-4 hours | Highlights: Panoramic views, cultural immersion

Day 4: Valley Trek → Ouzoud → Marrakech

Final morning trek through apple orchards and agricultural terraces. Drive to Ouzoud to visit Morocco's famous waterfalls (optional). Return to Marrakech in the late afternoon, arriving around 6:00 PM.

Trekking: 2-3 hours | Optional: Ouzoud Waterfalls visit

What to Bring

  • Comfortable trekking shoes (trail runners acceptable)
  • Light daypack (20-30L)
  • Layers for variable weather (light jacket, fleece)
  • Sun protection (hat, sunglasses, sunscreen)
  • Water bottle (1.5L capacity)
  • Camera for cultural and landscape photography
  • Small gifts for host families (optional: tea, sweets)
  • Personal toiletries and medications

Know Before You Go

  • Altitude: Valley elevation 1,800-2,200m. No altitude concerns.
  • Difficulty: Moderate - suitable for anyone with basic fitness.
  • Cultural sensitivity: Dress modestly (shoulders and knees covered). Ask permission before photographing people.
  • Language: Local Berber (Tamazight) spoken. Guides speak English/French.
  • Connectivity: Limited mobile signal in the valley.

Fitness Requirements

This trek is rated Moderate. You should be able to:

  • Walk 3-5 hours per day at a leisurely pace
  • Handle gentle ascents and descents
  • Adapt to basic accommodation (shared facilities)
  • No previous trekking experience required

Accommodation & Food

Accommodation: 3 nights in traditional Berber gîtes (family-run guesthouses). Rooms are simple but clean, with shared bathrooms. Mattresses and blankets provided.

Meals: All meals included from lunch Day 1 to lunch Day 4. Enjoy authentic home-cooked Berber cuisine including tagines, couscous, fresh bread, seasonal vegetables, and mint tea. Vegetarian options always available.

Climate & Best Season

Best time: April to October (pleasant valley climate)

Temperature range:

  • Spring/Autumn: 15-25°C daytime, 5-10°C nighttime
  • Summer: 25-35°C daytime, 10-15°C nighttime
  • Winter: Possible but colder (0-15°C)
