Ouzoud Waterfalls & Bin El Ouidane Escape

3 Days Easy Middle Atlas & Lakes

Trip Overview

A refreshing journey from Marrakech to North Africa’s highest waterfalls and the stunning turquoise waters of Bin El Ouidane lake. Perfect for nature lovers and families seeking a mix of relaxation and soft adventure in the Middle Atlas.

Experience the thunderous beauty of Ouzoud, meet local barbary macaques, and enjoy the serene landscape of Morocco's most famous lake. This tour combines guided nature walks with boat rides and lakeside serenity.

Highlights

  • Visit North Africa’s highest waterfalls (Ouzoud)
  • Guided hike through olive groves and valley floors
  • Full day of lake activities at Bin El Ouidane
  • Boat trip on the turquoise waters of the dam
  • Natural limestone bridge of Imi N’Ifri
  • Scenic Middle Atlas mountain drives

Detailed Itinerary

Day 1: Marrakech – Ouzoud Waterfalls (Middle Atlas beauty)

Morning departure from Marrakech heading northeast through the Haouz plains (approx. 2.5 hours). Arrival at Ouzoud Waterfalls. Guided walking tour from the top to the bottom through olive groves. Lunch at a traditional restaurant overlooking the waterfalls. Evening calm after day visitors leave.

Overnight: Traditional gîte or rural lodge in Ouzoud village.

Day 2: Ouzoud – Bin El Ouidane Lake (Turquoise magic)

Short mountain drive via the village of Ait Waouzgit to the famous Bin El Ouidane dam. Full day by the lake. Ideal for water activities: speedboat ride, kayaking, or swimming in clear turquoise waters surrounded by Atlas mountains.

Overnight: Lakeside hotel with sunset views.

Day 3: Bin El Ouidane – Imi N’Ifri Bridge – Marrakech

Departure from the lake toward Demnate to visit Imi N’Ifri, a massive natural rock bridge carved by water over centuries. Return to Marrakech via mountain roads passing Sidi Rahal, arriving early evening.

Know Before You Go

  • Season: Excellent year-round; summer is great for lake activities.
  • Clothing: Comfortable walking shoes and swimwear are essential.
  • Water: Stay hydrated; the Middle Atlas can be warm in the sun.
  • Wildlife: Respect the local monkeys at Ouzoud; do not feed them processed foods.
